Methodology of Analysis

Which categories are considered fake?

  • Final Report False - Filed after confirming bogus report.
  • Mistake of Fact or of Law or Civil Dispute - Filed after confirming exaggeration or misinterpretation of incident, or a false allegation filed over a civil dispute.

Final reports of any category are filed by police with approval from court. For more information about final reports, refer to sections of CrPC (Criminal Procedure Code) related to case disposal.

FAQs

1. Why are only 3 categories taken for estimation?

Other categories in disposals (withdrawals, acquittals due to insufficient evidence, etc.) do not reach a solid conclusion. Thus, they’re not taken for estimation.

2. TheNewsMinute article on false cases by Geetika Mantri

This article, which claims that false cases are rare, is often brought up in discussions.

The article presents 2020 NCRB data that is confined to police disposal, and lacks proper research on police case disposal terminology. It assumes that 5 categories fall under 'false'.

It goes on to make a biased statistical interpretation that every other case including cases concluded with insufficient evidence involve true incidents, whereas in reality they are unknown sets of data that can, at best, be estimated based on cases concluded with proof.

More

It often refers to withdrawn cases throughout the article which come under court disposal data from the NCRB report, which has been ignored by the author.

A reference is made to a survey from 2014 (8 years before authoring of this article) that only covers Delhi jurisdiction, and makes a sampling bias by applying a survey that has one city as sample to the whole country (a heterogenous unobserved dataset), drawing baseless implication that false cases are mostly filed by parents of the woman.

Whereas reports of individual incidents from credible news outlets and men's rights sources involve the woman herself filing false reports in majority of the cases.

3. If false cases get resolved quickly, wouldn’t they accumulate more than convicted ones?

The genuineness of a case has no effect on how quickly it reaches resolution.

Two strongest factors that quicken case conclusion:

  • Solid evidence
  • Strength of legal defense or prosecution, which requires more resources
