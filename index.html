Methodology of Analysis

More about methodology ▼

Which categories are considered fake?

  • Final Report False
    Filed after confirming bogus report.
  • Mistake of Fact or of Law or Civil Dispute
    Filed after confirming exaggeration or misinterpretation of incident by complainant, or false allegation filed over a civil dispute.

Final reports of any category are filed by police with approval from court.

Which categories are considered true incidents?

  • Cases Convicted - Convictions are made after proving guilt of accused.

Faulty convictions later acquitted are ignored due to rare occurence and lack of proper and relevant data.

How is estimate calculated?

Estimated share of fake cases is calculated as percentage of total cases that have reached solid conclusions.

\[ Fake\ Cases\ \% = \frac{Fake\ Cases}{Fake\ Cases + Convicted\ Cases} \times 100 \]

Frequently Asked Questions

1. Why are only 3 categories taken for estimation?

Other categories in disposals (withdrawals, acquittals due to insufficient evidence, etc.) do not reach a solid conclusion. Thus, they’re not taken for estimation.

2. TheNewsMinute article on false cases by Geetika Mantri

This article, which claims that false cases are rare, presents 2020 NCRB data that is confined to police disposal. It lacks proper research on terminology and assumes that false cases only fall under FRF.

It includes cases from previous years under investigation into the whole set of data, but only keeps cases closed as FRF in 2020 in the false case set.

It goes on to make a biased statistical interpretation that every other case including cases concluded with insufficient evidence involve true incidents, whereas in reality they are unknown sets of data that can, at best, be estimated based on cases concluded with proof.

The article in question has interpreted the data through a strong lens of confirmation bias.

More on the article ▼

It often refers to withdrawn cases throughout the article which come under court disposal, which has been ignored by the author. There is no Final Report filed for withdrawal of complaints.

A reference is made to a survey from 2014 (8 years before authoring of this article) that only covers Delhi jurisdiction, and makes a sampling bias by applying a survey that has one city as sample to the whole country (a heterogenous dataset), drawing faulty implication that false cases are mostly filed by parents of the woman.

Whereas reports of individual incidents from credible news outlets and men's rights sources involve the woman herself filing false reports in majority of the cases.

3. If false cases get resolved quickly, wouldn’t they accumulate more than convicted ones?

The genuineness of a case has no effect on how quickly it reaches resolution.

Two strongest factors that quicken case conclusion:

  • Solid evidence
  • Strength of legal defense or prosecution, which requires more resources
